# Final Recommendation

## 1. Factors most strongly associated with monthly sales
Regression analysis indicates that footfall, marketing spend, inventory availability, staff count, store type, and customer ratings have the strongest associations with monthly sales.

## 2. Variables leadership should focus on
- Footfall (β=27.32, p<0.001)
- Marketing spend (β=1.21, p<0.001)
- Inventory availability (β=3062, p<0.001)
- Staff count (β=3514, p=0.003)
- Store format strategy (Airport and Mall stores outperform)
- Customer experience (customer rating)

## 3. Variables that should not be over-interpreted
- Average discount percentage (p=0.225)
- South region effect (p=0.536)
- Regression intercept

## 4. Recommended business actions
- Increase investment in customer acquisition and footfall generation.
- Maintain high inventory availability.
- Optimize staffing levels.
- Expand high-performing store formats such as Airport and Mall locations.
- Continue targeted marketing investments.

## 5. Risks and limitations
- Approximately 14.3% of sales variation remains unexplained.
- External factors such as weather, local events, promotions, and management quality are not captured.
- Results are sensitive to data quality and model assumptions.

## 6. Why regression does not prove causation
Regression identifies statistical associations, not causal relationships. Significant variables may correlate with unobserved factors. Experimental or quasi-experimental methods are required to establish causality.
